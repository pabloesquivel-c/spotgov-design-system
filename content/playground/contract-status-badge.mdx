Right now contract status is buried in a table column with no visual weight.
The goal here is a small, glanceable badge that carries enough meaning on its
own: color, label, and icon together, so a reviewer can scan a list of
contracts and immediately spot what needs attention.

## B1: Filled vs. stroke

First pass: does a solid fill or an outlined badge read better against a
dense table row? Toggle between the two below.

<DemoFrame
  variants={[
    { label: 'Filled', content: <Badge.Root variant='filled' color='blue'>Awarded</Badge.Root> },
    { label: 'Stroke', content: <Badge.Root variant='stroke' color='blue'>Awarded</Badge.Root> },
  ]}
/>

Filled won. It holds up better at a glance in a dense table, where a thin
outline tends to disappear next to other borders.

## B2: Status palette

Each lifecycle stage gets its own color, pulled straight from AlignUI's
status roles rather than inventing new ones.

<SwatchGrid
  swatches={[
